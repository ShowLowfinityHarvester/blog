American Eagle CRJ700 Crashes Into The Potomac River After Colliding With Military Helicopter On Short Final In D.C

WSLF Radio News Jan 30, 2025 - Updated Feb 17, 2025

â€‹On January 29, 2025, a mid-air collision occurred near Ronald Reagan Washington National Airport involving American Eagle Flight 5342, a Bombardier CRJ700, and a U.S. Army Sikorsky UH-60 Black Hawk helicopter. Tragically, all 67 individuals aboard both aircraft lost their lives. This incident marked the deadliest U.S. air disaster since 2001.

Investigation Findings

The National Transportation Safety Board is conducting a thorough investigation into the collision, and have released a preliminary report. Their findings revealed several critical factors:â€‹

Altitude Deviation

The Black Hawk helicopter was authorized to operate at or below 200 feet above mean sea level but was found to be flying between 300 and 350 feet at the time of the collision.

Communication Issues

The helicopter crew reported visual contact with the CRJ and intended to maintain separation. However, they may have missed parts of air traffic control transmissions due to simultaneous radio communications.

Airspace Complexity

The proximity of helicopter routes to the approach path of Runway 33 at Reagan National Airport posed significant risks. The NTSB noted that helicopters flying designated routes had minimal vertical separation from landing aircraft, leading to frequent collision avoidance alerts over the past decade.

Safety Recommendations and Actions

In response to the findings, the NTSB issued urgent safety recommendations, including helicopter route restrictions and enhanced ATC procedures. The Federal Aviation Administration has implemented temporary restrictions on helicopter flights in the vicinity of Reagan National Airport and is evaluating the NTSB's recommendations for permanent changes. As the aviation community continues to mourn, efforts are underway to implement safety measures aimed at preventing such tragedies in the future.â€‹

Memorials and Community Impact

The collision profoundly affected communities nationwide, especially in Wichita, Kansas, home to many victims. Memorial services and vigils were held to honor those lost, highlighting the deep sorrow felt by families, friends, and colleagues.
